Actually, THz band signal between 30km-36000km will be highly influenced by ionospheric plasma. Below are some important factors about it.

Near Earth Channel Model:

In this part, authors describe how THz band fade in the near earth orbit.

Plasma Frequency: The plasma frequency is a measure of oscillation frequency of free electrons and ions due to the Coulomb force in the electric field and can be expressed as

$$ f_{pe}=\frac{e}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{n_e}{\epsilon_em_e}} $$

$n_e$ denotes the electron density in the ionosphere.

$e=1.6021 \times 10^{-19}$ is coulombs in the electron charge.

$m_e=9.109 \times 10_{-31} kg$ is the mass of a single electron.

$\epsilon_0=8.854 \times 10^{-12} F/m$ is the vacuum permittivity.

Collision Frequency: The interactions among charged electrons, ions, and neutral particles also generate collisions. A measure for the intensity of collisions is the collision frequency.

$$ f_{ce} = v_{ei}+v_{en}+v_{in} $$

Each term can be expressed as,

$$ v_{ei} = [24+4.18log_{10}(\frac{T^3}{n_e})]n_eT^{-3/2}, \v_{en}=5.4\times10^{-10}n_nT^{1/2}, \ v_{in}=2.6\times 10^{-9}(n_e+n_n)W^{-1/2} $$

$ν$ denotes the frequency, with subscripts $ei$, $en$, and $in$ representing the collisions of electrons with ions, electrons with neutral particles, and ions with neutral particles, respectively.

$T$ denotes the temperature (with an average value of 1000 K in ionosphere).

$W$ denotes the mass of ions and neutral particles (with an equal value assumed to be $1.67493 × 10−27 kg$).

$n_n$ denotes the density of neutral particles with a peak value of approximately $3×10^5/cm^3$

Depolarization: The ionosphere also causes depolarization of EM waves, known as the Faraday rotation. Such depolarization angle over a displacement $s$ is expressed as,

$$ d\phi_{FR}/ds =2.36\times10^4\overline{B}_Gf_c^{-2}n_e $$

$\overline{B}_G$ is the average Earth magnetic field intensity with a range of 25 to 65 μT.

$f_c$ is the carrier frequency.

Attenuation Factor: The attenuation factor, which is the loss per unit distance along signal’s propagation path, is a function of both collision frequency $f_{ce}$ and plasma frequency $f_{pe}$, can be expressed as:

$$ L_i=\frac{1}{2}k(\frac{f_{pe}}{f_{c}})^2\frac{f_{ce}}{f_{c}}(1+\frac{1}{2}(\frac{f_{pe}}{f_{c}})^2) $$

$k$ is the wavenumber.

The attenuation factor monotonously decreases with the increase of signal frequency, and lower RF bands are subject to higher attenuation, as compared to the THz band.

Orbit Perturbation Modeling and Beam Tracking for Small Satellites

Gravitational Effect:

When a pair of identical small satellites (denoted as a leader and a follower for differentiation) locate in the same circular orbit with a relatively small distance (usually much smaller than the geocentric distance of the orbit), as shown in Fig. 6, their respective equations of motion per unit mass, or perturbative accelerations $\ddot{r}_{F/L}$, are

$$ \ddot{r}{F}=-\mu\frac{r_F}{||r_F||^3}+f_F \ \ddot{r}{L}=-\mu\frac{r_L}{||r_L||^3}+f_L $$

$μ$ is the standard gravitational parameter

$r_{F/L} = [x_{F/L}y_{F/L}z_{F/L}]^T$ denotes the position vector and $f_{F/L}$ denotes other perturbation forces on the follower or leader, respectively.

The relative motion of the follower with respect to the leader under general perturbations can be expressed as,

$$ \ddot{p} = \ddot{r}{F} - \ddot{r}{L} \ \ddot{p} = -\mu\frac{r_L+p}{||r_L+p||^3}+\mu\frac{r_L}{||r_L||^3}+f_F-f_L $$

Upper function shows a closed-form solution for the relative position of the follower with respect to the leader as a function of time.

$\dot{x}$ and $\dot{y}$ denote the velocities along local directions.

$\omega=\sqrt{\mu/r^3}$ is the orbit velocity.

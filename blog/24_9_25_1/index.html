Solutions: Lyapunov + transitional method

Below is its experiment setup, which is useful.

Inclination of 87°; 1070 km low Earth orbit satellite constellation; 192 satellites distributed over 16 orbits, with 12 satellites per orbital plane; The simulation runs for 3600 seconds, with each time slot lasting 1 second, used for making satellite peer offloading decisions. The computing power of the satellite is 2 Gcycles/s. Each satellite En has a local computing capability of 6 tasks per time slot. The transmission rate of inter-satellite links is uniformly distributed between [100M, 300M] bit/s. The inter-satellite transmission power is 0.1 W, and the propagation delay between adjacent satellites is calculated as the straight-line distance divided by the speed of light.

Satellite-Terrestrial Assisted Multi-Tier Computing Networks With MIMO Precoding and Computation Optimization

A dense satellite and ground integrated multi-layer computing network architecture, with different computing densities at each layer, to better approximate the multi-layer computing process. Utilizing multiple satellites to multiplex spectrum resources and employing MIMO precoding to suppress co-channel interference.

Then, by jointly optimizing the UT-satellite association, MIMO precoding for both UT and satellites, the computational depth at the local and satellite layers, and the allocation of computational resources at each layer, a weighted sum energy consumption minimization problem is formulated.

Then, the optimization variables are solved iteratively, where UT-satellite association is obtained with closed-form solution, the computing depth of local/satellitetier is solved with first-order Taylor expansion. Then, quadratic transform based fractional programming is used to solve MIMO transmit precoding subproblem. Finally, the computation resource allocation at the local tier and the satellite tier is derived in closed-form expressions and the computation resource allocation at the GW can be dealt with the interior point method.

Cooperative Federated Learning Over Ground-to-Satellite Integrated Networks: Joint Local Computation and Data Offloading (GOOD GOOD Paper)

Mobile Communication Across Remote Areas: Although mobile communication systems have been widely deployed in current terrestrial networks, there are still many isolated areas on Earth lacking developed communication infrastructure (such as rural and maritime regions). Since these areas have neither servers nor access points, it is currently challenging to conduct Federated Learning (FL) across devices located in these regions.

(i) An edge computing unit for processing data offloaded from the ground users

(ii) A server for aggregating the models within each cluster

(iii) A relay for sharing the model/data with other satellites via inter-satellite links (ISLs)

Compared to traditional terrestrial communication networks, one of the key challenges faced by integrated terrestrial-satellite networks is the continuous movement of satellites in orbit, which limits the coverage time of each satellite over any given cluster. To address this problem, we suggest allowing each satellite to transfer its trained model and the data offloaded from clients to its neighboring satellite (which will soon cover the same area) through Inter-Satellite Links (ISL).

As for the issue of satellite batteries, they only have the capacity for efficient computing when the sun is shining on them.

Solutions: Iterative Algorithm, block-coordinated descent

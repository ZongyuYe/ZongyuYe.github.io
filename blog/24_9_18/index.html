Four kinds of typical satellites:

  1. GEO (Geostationary Earth Orbit): About 36,000 kilometers.
  2. MEO (Middle Earth Orbit): The orbital altitude is between 2000 and 36,000 km. (GPS)
  3. LEO (Low Earth Orbit): The orbital altitude is about 600-2000 km.
  4. VLEO (Very Low Earth Orbit): Nearly 350 km. Outlines:

Space-air-ground network initially provide comprehensive basic network connection, ensuring network availability in areas not covered by terrestrial networks. In the next step, services need heavy traffic are introduced to provide better service. Meanwhile Satellite network (Or NTN, Non-Terrestrial Network) will release some pressure on the ground network. Finally, it will offload intelligence tasks to NTN to realize computing power (force) offloading.

Communication of space-air-ground network:

  1. Public Safety and Industry Disaster Recovery Emergency Communications: Emergency satellite communications are primarily used for disaster relief, public security emergencies, large-scale security, industry disaster recovery, and other emergency events for on-site command and dispatch and remote data backup processing. In the field of industry emergency, off-site disaster recovery, data protection, and recovery for special industries will also become important growth points for future industrialized emergency communications.

  2. Remote Areas, and Comprehensive Coverage Over Sea, Land, and Air: Regarding coverage in remote areas: The construction cost of connecting the last 1% of the population with ground broadband is 40 times the average cost of connecting the first 95%. Thanks to the low latency and wide coverage characteristics of low Earth orbit (LEO) satellites, it will be possible in the future to establish a ground-to-space communication link between miniaturized satellite relay stations in remote areas and the LEO satellite constellation in a “base station-free” manner. Users in remote areas without ground operator base station coverage, such as wilderness and mountainous areas, can achieve smooth communication access. In terms of maritime affairs on the high seas: Satellite communication deployment on ships, led by space-based communication, can effectively realize communication services such as message sending and receiving between “ship-to-ship” and “ship-to-shore”, and voice dispatching.

  3. Airborne aviation: Airborne satellite communication can provide cabin passengers with high-speed, real-time, and smooth Internet connection services, improving the passenger flight experience. It will become an important infrastructure for the digital and intelligent development of civil aviation.

Connection of space-air-ground network:

  1. Mobile Terminal Direct-to-Satellite Services: With the mobile phone being the most commonly used personal communication tool, the technology enabling direct-to-satellite connections will allow for more flexible and convenient access to both terrestrial and space networks. In the absence of ground networks or when ground networks are compromised, users can access the satellite network directly via their mobile phones, achieving true anytime, anywhere, on-demand access. This capability significantly supports personal users in maintaining network connectivity during outdoor adventures, large sporting events, severe weather conditions, and in remote areas. (Car-mounted satellite communication services)
  2. Unmanned Operations: In terms of offshore unmanned operations, with the development of the maritime economy, unmanned vessels, intelligent robots, and other equipment will gradually be applied to marine operations. Through remote real-time remote control or preset control programs, they will replace human workers in performing specific tasks. For example, using aquatic robots to help ships clean various barnacles and other microorganisms, using unmanned ships to replace manual work in tasks such as channel patrol, and underwater topography mapping of waterways. Satellite internet can provide more stable network support for offshore unmanned operations, improve operational efficiency, such as using video data transmission and AI visual analysis technology, real-time analysis of the operational status of ships and marine conditions, achieving intelligent management of ships. In the aspect of industrial unmanned operations: With the development of industrial digital transformation, the industry gradually uses drones or robots and other automation equipment to perform tasks in the industrial field, including inspections, monitoring, maintenance, logistics, etc. Industrial unmanned operations often need to be carried out in vast areas, and traditional wireless communication networks sometimes cannot cover remote areas, oceans, and other regions. However, the wide coverage and high-speed data transmission capabilities of satellite internet can meet the requirements of industrial unmanned operations for real-time and high reliability. For example, devices such as drones or robots can use satellite signals to locate their positions and transmit real-time location information back to the ground control center. This enables the tracking and monitoring of device status and operational trajectories, efficiently supporting operational decisions and adjustments.
  3. Enterprise Remote Asset Management: Enterprises with assets widely distributed in remote areas, such as oil fields in the Gobi Desert, hydropower stations in deep mountains, equipment and goods in far-sea transportation, often have needs for asset monitoring and management. However, due to the difficulty of covering these areas with ground networks, enterprises face the dilemma of high labor inspection costs and low efficiency. Satellite communication networks, by integrating satellite-borne sensing data and edge computing capabilities, can provide industries with asset management services such as fault prediction, low-latency alarms, remote equipment shutdown, control, and remote technical guidance. For example, through satellite IoT, scattered minerals, oil wells, and high-value raw materials and bulk commodities in transit around the world can be connected to the enterprise’s regulatory network. This allows for timely reporting of enterprise asset movements and statuses, achieving enterprise remote monitoring and digital management.
  4. Precision Agriculture Operations.

For non-sensitive remote sensing data, it can be applied in fields such as cultural tourism, game scene modeling, streaming media content production, and geographic education, to leverage the value of digital content.
  3. Relying on an integrated terrestrial and celestial intelligent perception network, combined with technologies such as AI, blockchain, computing power, cloud, and big data, it is possible to achieve “comprehensive coverage, digitalization, and dynamic” monitoring and supervision of resources in fields such as the ocean, ecological environment, and agriculture. This further promotes the improvement of social intelligent supervision quality and efficiency.

The expected architecture of space-air-ground network:

https://www.zte.com.cn/content/dam/zte-site/res-www-zte-comcn/mediares/magazine/publication/com_cn/article/202305/3.pdf

The 6G network can construct a low-latency integrated satellite-terrestrial network system through low Earth orbit (LEO) satellite constellations. Compared to LEO satellites, geostationary orbit (GEO) satellites have some significant advantages in terms of coverage range, network complexity, and mobility management. Therefore, the integration of GEO and LEO satellites needs to be considered in the 6G integrated satellite-terrestrial network to achieve complementary advantages.

The 6G network architecture needs to integrate capabilities that are intelligent by design, computationally powerful, and inherently secure, to achieve ubiquitous connectivity, omnipresent computing power, and comprehensive intelligence.

With the continuous emergence of new services, satellite networks, acting as relays for remote ground data centers, can no longer provide efficient data processing responses. Hence, the integrated satellite-terrestrial communication network needs to consider edge computing capabilities, implementing a flexible division of edge computing between satellites and the ground. This approach places services with high globality, complexity, and low latency on ground network platforms, and services with high real-time requirements and low computational complexity on satellite nodes. Through on-orbit computing services, it reduces latency and achieves efficient service distribution. For instance, utilizing satellite pre-data caching and satellite broadcast/multicast functions can significantly reduce traffic on the forward link of the integrated satellite-terrestrial communication network; moving some satellite data processing (such as Earth imaging and weather observation) to satellites can greatly reduce the amount of data that needs to be sent back, saving bandwidth on the feeder link.

As the computing power of onboard electronic devices continues to develop, there has been a significant improvement in onboard computing capabilities. However, the network still needs to dynamically allocate and flexibly schedule computing resources, storage resources, and network resources among the cloud, network, and edge as needed. Through an intelligent collaborative mechanism for edge computing, an intelligent network orchestration system can match the best computing resources for users’ business needs based on network status and computing power status, and calculate the optimal path from the user to the target computing power node, determining the forwarding path for the service. Edge computing nodes deployed on satellites can utilize relatively limited computing and network resources to perform some edge computing tasks, while central computing nodes on the ground, with abundant computing and network resources, provide strong support for terminals and edges, capable of completing complex computing tasks that edge nodes cannot handle.

Firstly, efficient computing power requires a professional network. Focusing on dedicated scenarios can complete more calculations with lower power consumption and cost. At present, video and image analysis are relatively mature scenes with the greatest demand for computing power, which basically run through all intelligent scenes in all industries. Video analysis and processing at the Edge and in the Cloud requires high throughput, which depends on two key indicators, network bandwidth and latency. The larger the bandwidth and the lower the latency is, the greater the data throughput is. (Large amount of videos and image demands network with higher throughput.

Secondly, efficient computing power requires an elastic network. Such as the overallocation of computing power, the agility provided by computing power, and the elasticity of 100ms level light computing, elastic data processing requires the network to provide agile connection establishment, bandwidth and service level flexibility adjustment capabilities between data requirements and computing power resources.

Thirdly, efficient computing power requires a collaborative network. From the cooperation between multiple cores inside the processor, to the “balance of computing power” among multiple servers in the data centers, and then to the “on-demand computing power” of the entire network edge, the purpose of collaboration is to make full use of computing power resources, and the bearer network needs to support the balance of computing power, traffic scheduling and congestion management between multiple edges, between the edge and the core.

Three typical application scenarios about computing power network and space-air-ground network:

  1. A common scenario involves areas where terrestrial networks cannot provide coverage or are congested, requiring users to offload tasks, such as image super-resolution. In such instances, the decision whether to offload to LEO, VLEO, GEO, MEO, MEC, or the Cloud comes into play. At this juncture, several factors merit consideration, primarily focusing on user experience quality alongside the company’s profit and cost considerations.
  2. Another scenario involves distributed learning, such as when multiple geographically dispersed companies need to engage in distributed learning using their own data. In such cases, satellite networks can be employed for communication, not only offering higher efficiency but also reducing the likelihood of packet loss due to congestion. Simultaneously, if the learned parameters need to be retained within each company, satellite networks can also provide reliable, high-speed services for inference when required. Should there be a need to integrate these parameters to form a single model, it can also be deployed on satellites to offer services with a broader coverage. This exemplifies a typical scenario where Satellite Terrestrial Networks (STN) and Computing Power Networks (CPN) converge.
  3. The final scenario touches on entertainment and lifestyle, and can also relate to telemedicine. For example, imagine wanting to remotely experience the taste of a delicacy your friend is enjoying in the United States, or the sensation of touching the sandy beaches of Hawaii from the comfort of your home. This would require sensory interconnectivity. While short-distance sensory interconnectivity might be achievable through cloud services and terrestrial networks, the experience could deteriorate as distances increase due to latency in terrestrial networks. Hence, the use of satellite networks to facilitate communication becomes essential. Additionally, some sensory conversions might utilize AI models, necessitating computational power from satellites. Actually, STN can do better in remote areas and Long distance communication.

Mobility Management in Satellite

SKYCASTLE: Taming LEO Mobility to Facilitate Seamless and Low-latency Satellite Internet Services

https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10621390/?arnumber=10621390.

The fundamental challenge stems from a unique characteristic of ISTNs: not only users are mobile, but also core network infrastructures (i.e., LEO satellites) are frequently changing their locations in the network.

Research scope

Firstly, efficiently integrate satellite constellations into existing terrestrial Internet, emerging ISTNs like Starlink adopts IP-based networking architecture with heterogeneous wireless access technologies. (Integrated network)

Second, unlike link-layer solutions, a network-layer solution does not rely on any assumption about the underlying wireless access technologies. (Non-lower)

Finally, as compared to high-layer approaches (e.g., QUIC’s connection identifier), a network-layer solution achieves higher handover efficiency (e.g., shorter disruption time). (Non-higher)

Deploying anchor on the ground

Figure 4a and 4b plot the end-to-end connection uninterrupted ratio (CUR) and RTTs when anchors are deployed at GSs. Our experiment results reveal that deploying anchors on GSs can result in poor network continuity. As shown in Figure 4a, The server-to-user CUR is only 79% on average, indicating that the connection is unavailable for at least 20% of the entire session.

Deploying anchor in satellites

Figure 4a and 4b plots the end-to-end CUR and RTTs when users choose their initial ingress satellite as the anchor. We observe that deploying anchors on satellites can significantly improve the server-to-user CUR, but it still suffers from high user-perceived RTTs. Figure 4c plots the RTTs changing over time. As users roam around the world and satellites move around the earth, we observe that the RTT can increase by about 100ms over a period of 25 minutes.

Method Overview

  1. Judiciously deploying distributed anchors at LEO satellites to efficiently manage the mobility of both users and network infrastructures.
  2. Dynamically assigning satellite anchors to users to avoid triangular routing and attain low latency.

The anchor manager aims to maximize the time users are managed by the same anchor.

Dynamic Space-Ground Integrated Mobility Management Strategy for Mega LEO Satellite Constellations

https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10478861/?arnumber=10478861

Contributions:

  1. Dynamic management node decision-making (DMND) problem and unguaranteed delays: Due to the triple mobility between LEO satellites as well as MEO and GS management nodes, the management node of each LEO satellite needs to be changed constantly, which brings the DMND problem. Since the selection of GS management node or MEO management node has a large spatial-temporal scale difference, the handover delays are hard to be guaranteed. To ensure the effectiveness of the dynamic SGIMM architecture, minimizing the handover management delay is a pivotal goal to the DMND problem.

  2. Dynamic content migrations caused migration delays and overheads: With the change of management node, the buffered contents of registered users, gNB-DUs, and gNB-CU-UPs in the AMF and gNB-CU-CP functions should also be migrated to the new management node accordingly. This brings additional migration delays and overheads to DMND problem.

  3. Fluctuating storage overheads: As the overall network topology changes, the global non-uniform-distributed users will bring fluctuating and differential storage overheads to the storage-constrained MEO management nodes. The storage provided to the gNB-CU-CP and AMF functions in the MEO management node should be evaluated, and the practical MEO storage overhead need to be constrained in the DMND problem.

Handover Analysis in Ultra-Dense LEO Satellite Networks With Beamforming Methods

https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9946436/?arnumber=9946436

This paper is aim to improve the sum data rate and also improve the handover performance.

Contributions:

  1. A modified low-complexity matching algorithm and a novel handover rate analysis method are proposed. We develop a practical UDSGIN for traffic hotspot areas, where the matching relations between users and BSs are obtained by maximizing the sum rate of users. We add the retention and switching mechanism in the proposed matching algorithm to reduce the computational complexity. Combining the proposed matching algorithm and the pause time of users inside the cell boundaries, we derive the expressions of handover rate and handover failure rate under UDSGIN.
  2. A MPSO-based satellite beamforming method is proposed. In order to provide equal coverage for all LBSs, we present a MPSO-based beamforming method, which can generate arbitrarily shaped beams by the pre-defined antenna pattern. The amplitude and phase of each antenna array are obtained by the proposed method.

Maximize the sum rate of all users in the ground network under the constraint of space link backhaul capacity.

QoE-Driven Intelligent Handover for User-Centric Mobile Satellite Networks

https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9112229/?arnumber=9112229

Current handover methods are based on either signal strength or service time, however, due to the randomness of user terminal (UT) arrivals and the unbalanced traffic distribution for high-mobility satellite networks, the success rate can hardly be guaranteed.

Contributions:

  1. To maximize the UT′s QoE, we propose a QoE-driven Intelligent Handover (QIH) algorithm. The handover factors are first predicted according to the periodic motion of satellites. Then to make optimal handover decisions and achieve best QoE for candidate satellite, RL is adopted to solve the multi-criteria optimization problem.
  2. To avoid frequent satellite handover, they propose a spatial relationship coupling model to predict relative motion patterns between UTs and satellites. The relative azimuth and distance are quantified, and the longest service time of candidate satellites can be derived in advance.
  3. To improve the handover success rate, they develop an available channel estimation model. Taking advantage of the periodic movement of adjacent satellites, future available communication resources can be predicted to decrease the handover failure.

User Grouping-Based Beam Handover Scheme with Load-Balancing for LEO Satellite Networks

https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10436950/?arnumber=10436950

Specifically, on the one hand, current research focuses on single-beam satellite handover scenarios, which fail to align with actual satellite deployments and cannot adequately reduce the frequency of handover occurrences. On the other hand, a majority of the existing schemes primarily address scenarios involving a small or uniformly distributed user base. However, with the exponential growth in the number of users, the satellite decision making process becomes increasingly burdensome, especially when users with similar handover attributes are aggregated in a area, which will lead to many redundant satellite decisions.

(P.S. If a specific area has a lot of users gathering. Then decision making will be burdensome. )

Proposing a user grouping-based beam handover scheme with load-balancing. When faced with the multi-beam LEO satellite network scenario, users are grouped based on handover moment prediction, consider multiple factors in the process of group member selection, and make a uniform beam handover decision for users within the same group to reduce decision cost.

A Multi-Agent Deep Reinforcement Learning-Based Handover Scheme for Mega-Constellation Under Dynamic Propagation Conditions

https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10551688/?arnumber=10551688

Contributions:

  1. Different from the existing handover schemes where only the static propagation condition is considered, the dynamic propagation conditions between users and satellites are integrated into the proposed scheme design. Specifically, we first employ a three-state Markov model to characterize the typical propagation conditions including the LOS, shadowed, and blocked propagation states as well as the probabilistic transitions among the three states. Then, the Loo model is adopted to describe the time-varying LMS channels. In this way, the stochastic nature of the dynamic propagation conditions between users and satellites are sufficiently characterized and also put significant challenges in the satellite handover scheme design.
  2. Based on the characterization of dynamic propagation conditions between users and satellites, we design the frame structure and propose a centralized adaptive intelligent handover mechanism for LEO mega constellations, where the handover delay and handover failure probability are jointly considered. Then, we design the utility function for each user by taking the throughput of users and load-balancing demand of satellites into consideration. Moreover, we further formulate an optimization problem that aims at maximizing the long-term network overall utilities to optimize the performance of the proposed scheme.
  3. Although the formulated optimization problem can be characterized by a single-agent Markov decision process (MDP) framework and solved by the fundamental RL method, huge state and action space will be incurred, which will significantly increase the computation complexity and thus might not be appropriate for the LEO mega-constellations. To solve this problem, we convert the single-agent MDP framework to a cooperative multi-agent MDP framework, where the space and action space are efficiently reduced. Based on the constructed cooperative multi-agent MDP framework, we develop a MA-SHDQL algorithm, which can efficiently solve the formulated problem with relatively low complexity. In this way, the handover decisions can be intelligently determined by cognizing the dynamic propagation environments between users and satellites.
  4. As the LEO mega-constellation includes a large number of satellites, the centralized handover scheme might incur heavy signaling overhead and computation burden to the control center. To address this issue, we further propose a distributed intelligent handover scheme, where each user can independently determine its own handover decisions only based on its local information. Due to the distributed manner, the complexity of the developed distributed scheme will not increase as the numbers of users and satellites grow. Consequently, the proposed distributed scheme is promising to be applied in the LEO satellite network with massive users and satellites.

Seamless Handover in LEO Based Non-Terrestrial Networks: Service Continuity and Optimization

https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9984697/?arnumber=9984697

Contributions:

  1. We utilize the CHO mechanism to enhance the handover success rate and optimize the target satellite selection in LEO-based NTN. We treat the CHO decisions in a certain service duration as a spatial-temporal NTN service capability optimization problem. Different from existing works, the objective of this paper is to enhance the service continuity for LEO-based NTN with seamless handover optimization.
  2. We describe the CHO configuration and execution process in detail. Then, the step of target node condition monitoring is extracted for optimization. A reward function, related to USL service time and service capability, is designed and the optimal target satellite selection algorithm is proposed to obtain the maximum CHO reward in each time slot.
  3. We further propose the SCG to describe the NTN performance based on the prediction of different possible CHO combinations. The impact of HO selection on NTN service continuity is analyzed using SCG. Then, the SCDP scheme is proposed to calculate CHO sequences for accessing users with optimal service continuity in service duration.

Privacy-Driven Security-Aware Task Scheduling Mechanism for Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks

https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10506576/?arnumber=10506576

Contributions:

This paper proposes a privacy-driven security-aware task scheduling mechanism for SAGIN, where the overall task completion time, energy consumption and privacy leakage risks are minimized, given the data size, computation resource demands and privacy levels of tasks. A content-driven task privacy evaluation model is formulated to classify the privacy level of tasks.

Privacy Threats:

  1. Usage Privacy: Usage privacy mainly refers to the usage patterns of clients utilizing the services provided by edge nodes. For example, by reading and analyzing the time pattern of electricity consumption revealed by the data on a family smart meter, a curious edge node can infer their private information, like when they are away from home.
  2. Location Privacy: In a traditional MEC scenario, a device’s location can be exposed through being aware of its access point by curious edge nodes like Wi-Fi hotspots and base stations. Specifically, since the coverage area of each MEC access node is usually very limited and well-defined, devices connected to these nodes can be presumed to be active in this specific area, thus leaking location privacy information.
  3. Data Privacy: The data produced from IoT applications encapsulate various aspects of living space. Some of them may contain sensitive information like personal phone numbers, preferences, and biographical information; while others may not, such as air carbon dioxide index, stock price, and social news.

Minimize the cost of delay, energy consumption and privacy leakage risks of the SAGIN-assisted computation offloading system.
